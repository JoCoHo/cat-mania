Welcome to Cat Mania!

What is Cat Mania?

We are the premier website for cat fanatics - or fanaticats - to come together and share our love for our four-legged, whiskered friends. Whether you're a crazy cat lady, or perhaps someone simply curious about felines, we provide an array of cat content and means to tell the world how much you adore kitties.

Why do we love cats?

Our feline pals are not just toys; it is scientifically proven that they provide human beings with joy and comfort - and, sometimes that joy and comfort can be intense!

Human relationship with cats go back thousands of years, beginning almost 10,000 years ago. The ancient Egyptians were fond of cats, even believing that they possessed divine energy. Although cats are mostly kept as pets today, humans originally kept them around as a form of pest control, as cats are known to hunt rodents. Starting in the 19th century, cats began to be kept as pets, rather than just pest control; humans now valued their company more than their utility.

Cats can help people, particularly their masters, in many ways. They can help relieve loneliness, provide feelings of affection, and make us feel comfortable. They are also able to reduce stress, as a positive relationship with a cat releases oxytocin, a stress suppressor. One survey found that those who spend time with cats feel calmer and less upset than those who don't. Benefits of owning cats can even come in physical ways; one study found that cat owners have a lower risk of heart attacks than those who do not own cats.

Clearly, there are many reasons to adore our kitties. They don't just make us feel good, they are known to make us feel better!

Fun cat facts!

  • Cats don't just have whiskers on their face; they also have whiskers on the back of their front legs!
  • Female cats can become pregnant at surprisingly young ages, as early as 4 - 6 months of age.
  • Cats are massive sleepyheads! They will usually sleep for an astounding average of 15 hours every day.
  • Our cats are closely related to tigers! They both share around 95.6% of their genetic makeup.
  • Cats only meow when they want to communicate with humans. For example, when they want to be fed.
  • Known for being flexible, cats have 53 loosely fitting vertebrae in their spine, where as humans have only 34.
